Q.5: Reconciliation of Cost and Financial Accounts

Problem: Profit disclosed by a company's cost accounts for the year was 50,000/- whereas the net profit as disclosed by the financial accounts was 46,000/-. Following information is available:

  • a) Overheads as per cost accounts were estimated 55,555/-. The charge for the year shown by the financial accounts was 50,000/-.
  • b) Director's fees shown in financial accounts only 1,000/-.
  • c) The company allocated 2,000/- as provision for doubtful debts.
  • d) In financial accounts, depreciation was charged more in comparison with cost accounts by 3,000/-.
  • e) Share-transfer fees received during the year 445/-.
  • f) Provision for Income-Tax was 4,000/-.

Prepare a statement reconciling the figures shown by the cost and financial accounts.

Solution: Reconciliation Statement

Particulars Amount (₹) Amount (₹)
Net Profit as per Cost Accounts 50,000
Add: Incomes/Gains not recorded in Cost or Over-recoveries
1. Overheads over-recovered in Cost A/c (55,555 - 50,000) 5,555
2. Share-transfer fees received (Income in Financial A/c only) 445 6,000
56,000
Less: Expenses/Losses charged only in Financial A/c
1. Director's fees 1,000
2. Provision for doubtful debts 2,000
3. Excess depreciation charged in Financial A/c 3,000
4. Provision for Income-Tax 4,000 (10,000)
Net Profit as per Financial Accounts 46,000

Q.6: Reconciliation Statement

Problem: Profit disclosed by a Company's cost accounts for the year ended 31st March, 2014, was 1,00,000 whereas the net profit as disclosed by the financial accounts was 59,500. Following information is available:

  • a) Work was commenced on a new factory and expenditure of 60,000 was incurred. Depreciation was provided at 5% for six months in the financial accounts.
  • b) Directors Fees shown in financial accounts 4,000.
  • c) Share transferred fees received during the year was 2,000.
  • d) Provision for income tax was 30,000.
  • e) The Company allocated 10,000 as provision for doubtful debts.
  • f) Overheads as per cost accounts were estimated at 17,000. The charge for the year shown by the financial accounts was 14,000.

Solution: Reconciliation Statement

Working Note:

  • Depreciation on new factory: 60,000 × 5% × (6/12) = ₹ 1,500. This is charged only in Financial Accounts, so it reduces financial profit.
  • Overheads Over-recovered: Cost accounts (17,000) - Financial accounts (14,000) = ₹ 3,000. This increases cost profit, so it must be added back to match financial profit.
Particulars Amount (₹) Amount (₹)
Net Profit as per Cost Accounts 1,00,000
Add: Items increasing Financial Profit
1. Overheads over-recovered in Cost A/c (17,000 - 14,000) 3,000
2. Share transfer fees received (Income in Financial A/c only) 2,000 5,000
1,05,000
Less: Items decreasing Financial Profit
1. Depreciation on new factory (Financial A/c only) 1,500
2. Directors Fees 4,000
3. Provision for Income Tax 30,000
4. Provision for doubtful debts 10,000 (45,500)
Net Profit as per Financial Accounts 59,500

Q.13: Cost Sheet & Selling Price Calculation

Problem: A factory produces 100 units of a commodity. Cost of production is: Direct materials: ₹10,000, Direct Wages: ₹5,000, Direct Expenses: ₹1,000, Factory Overheads: ₹6,500, Administrative Overheads: ₹3,480. Profit of 25% on sales is to be realized. Prepare the cost sheet and find selling price of each commodity.

Solution: Cost Sheet (Output: 100 Units)

Particulars Total Cost (₹) Cost Per Unit (₹)
Direct Materials 10,000 100.00
Direct Wages 5,000 50.00
Direct Expenses 1,000 10.00
Prime Cost 16,000 160.00
Add: Factory Overheads 6,500 65.00
Works Cost 22,500 225.00
Add: Administrative Overheads 3,480 34.80
Cost of Production / Total Cost 25,980 259.80
Add: Profit (1/3 of Cost) 8,660 86.60
Sales 34,640 346.40

Working Note (Profit Calculation):

  • Profit is desired at 25% on Sales.
  • We know: If Profit is 1/4 (25%) on Sales, it equals 1/3 (33.33%) on Cost.
  • Profit = 1/3 of ₹25,980 = ₹8,660.
  • Selling price of each commodity = Total Sales / 100 units = 34,640 / 100 = ₹346.40.
