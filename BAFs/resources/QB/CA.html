Q.2: Cost Sheet with Per Unit Cost

Problem: Krishna Ltd. started a factory in Mumbai. Detail available for year ended 31st March, 2025: [cite: 1508, 1509]
Raw material consumed: 40,000 units @ Rs. 7 per unit. [cite: 1510]
Direct wages: Skilled worker Rs. 9 per unit, Unskilled worker Rs. 6 per unit. [cite: 1511, 1512]
Direct expenses: Rs. 3 per unit. [cite: 1513]
Work overheads: Rs. 8 per machine hours worked (25,000 hrs). [cite: 1514]
Offices overheads: Rs. 4,00,000. Sales commission: Rs. 4 per unit sold. [cite: 1515]
Units produced: 40,000. Units sold: 36,000. Selling price: Rs. 50 per unit. [cite: 1516, 1517]
Prepare cost sheet showing various elements of cost and per unit cost. [cite: 1518]

Solution: Cost Sheet of Krishna Ltd.

Output Produced: 40,000 units | Units Sold: 36,000 units

Particulars Total Cost (₹) Cost Per Unit (₹)
Raw Material Consumed (40,000 x ₹7) 2,80,000 7.00
Direct Wages: Skilled (40,000 x ₹9) 3,60,000 9.00
Direct Wages: Unskilled (40,000 x ₹6) 2,40,000 6.00
Direct Expenses (40,000 x ₹3) 1,20,000 3.00
Prime Cost 10,00,000 25.00
Add: Works Overheads (25,000 hrs x ₹8) 2,00,000 5.00
Works Cost 12,00,000 30.00
Add: Office Overheads 4,00,000 10.00
Cost of Production (for 40,000 units) 16,00,000 40.00
Less: Closing Stock of Finished Goods (4,000 units x ₹40) (1,60,000) -
Cost of Goods Sold (COGS) (for 36,000 units) 14,40,000 40.00
Add: Selling Overheads (Sales Comm. 36,000 x ₹4) 1,44,000 4.00
Cost of Sales 15,84,000 44.00

Working Notes:

  • Closing Stock Valuation: Units produced (40,000) - Units sold (36,000) = Closing Stock (4,000 units). It is valued at the Cost of Production per unit (₹40). Value = 4,000 x 40 = ₹1,60,000. [cite: 1516]
  • Selling Overheads: Sales commission is paid only on units sold. Hence, 36,000 units x ₹4 = ₹1,44,000. [cite: 1515, 1516]

Q.3: Statement of Cost (Reverse Calculation)

Problem: Books of Anand Mfg Co. for Aug 2014 show: [cite: 1519, 1520]
Direct labour cost: 16,000 (160% of factory overhead) [cite: 1521]
Cost of goods sold: 56,000 [cite: 1521]
Inventories (Op | Cl): RM (8,000 | 8,600), WIP (8,000 | 12,000), FG (14,000 | 18,000) [cite: 1523]
Selling Exp: 3,400, Gen & Admin Exp: 2,600, Sales: 75,000 [cite: 1523]
Prepare statement showing cost of goods manufactured, sold and profit. [cite: 1524]

Solution: Statement of Cost & Profit

Working Note (Reverse Calculations to find Purchases):

  • Factory Overhead: Direct Labour is 160% of Factory OH. Therefore, Factory OH = 16,000 / 1.60 = ₹10,000. [cite: 1521]
  • Cost of Production (COP): COGS (56,000) = COP + Op FG (14,000) - Cl FG (18,000). Thus, COP = 56,000 + 4,000 = ₹60,000. [cite: 1521, 1523]
  • Gross Works Cost: Net Works Cost (COP) (60,000) = Gross Works Cost + Op WIP (8,000) - Cl WIP (12,000). Thus, Gross Works Cost = 60,000 + 4,000 = ₹64,000. [cite: 1523]
  • Prime Cost: Gross Works Cost (64,000) = Prime Cost + Factory OH (10,000). Thus, Prime Cost = ₹54,000.
  • Raw Material Consumed: Prime Cost (54,000) = RM Consumed + Direct Labour (16,000). Thus, RM Consumed = ₹38,000. [cite: 1521]
  • Purchases: RM Consumed (38,000) = Op RM (8,000) + Purchases - Cl RM (8,600). Thus, Purchases = 38,000 - 8,000 + 8,600 = ₹38,600. [cite: 1523]
